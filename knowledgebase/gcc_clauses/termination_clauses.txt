================================================================================
                        GENERAL CONDITIONS OF CONTRACT (GCC)
                              TERMINATION CLAUSES
================================================================================

Document Reference: GCC-TERM-2026-v1.0
Last Updated: January 1, 2026
Applicable to: All Construction, Infrastructure & Engineering Projects
Jurisdiction: India (subject to Indian Contract Act, 1872)


SECTION 1: GENERAL PRINCIPLES OF TERMINATION
================================================================================

1.1 RIGHT TO TERMINATE
    Either Party may terminate the Contract only in accordance with the terms
    of this Termination Clause and applicable law. Termination shall be treated
    as an exceptional remedy and not exercised lightly.

1.2 TYPES OF TERMINATION
    a) Termination for Contractor Default (Employer’s convenience/right)
    b) Termination for Employer Default (Contractor’s right)
    c) Termination for Convenience (no fault of either party)
    d) Termination due to Prolonged Force Majeure
    e) Mutual Termination by Agreement

1.3 EFFECT OF TERMINATION
    Upon termination:
    - Work shall be stopped in an orderly manner.
    - Site, documents, and materials shall be handed over as provided herein.
    - Final accounts shall be prepared and settled in accordance with the
      settlement provisions of this Contract.
    - Termination shall not affect accrued rights and liabilities of the Parties.


SECTION 2: TERMINATION FOR CONTRACTOR DEFAULT
================================================================================

2.1 GROUNDS FOR TERMINATION BY EMPLOYER
    The Employer may terminate the Contract, in whole or in part, by written
    notice to the Contractor if any of the following occurs:

    a) Persistent Delay:
       - Failure to achieve key milestones or Time for Completion even after
         expiry of notice and cure period.
       - Cumulative delay attributable to Contractor exceeding the maximum
         permissible limit (e.g. 20–25% of original contract period).

    b) Serious Performance Failure:
       - Repeated failure to meet quality standards, specifications, or
         performance guarantees.
       - Failure to rectify major defects within reasonable time despite
         written instructions.

    c) Abandonment of Work:
       - Contractor suspends work without Employer’s consent for a continuous
         period (e.g. 14–21 days).
       - Contractor demobilizes key resources without justification.

    d) Insolvency / Bankruptcy:
       - Contractor becomes insolvent, enters into liquidation, or is subject
         to winding-up proceeding (other than for amalgamation/reconstruction).

    e) Unauthorised Subcontracting / Assignment:
       - Assignment of contract or substantial part of works without Employer’s
         prior written consent.

    f) Breach of Laws and Compliance:
       - Material violations of applicable laws, anti-bribery, anti-corruption,
         labour, tax, or environmental regulations.

    g) Safety and HSE Violations:
       - Repeated serious safety incidents or fatal accidents attributable to
         Contractor’s negligence, despite prior warnings.

    h) Fraud, Misrepresentation, or Corrupt Practices:
       - Evidence of fraud, collusion, or misrepresentation in bidding or
         contract performance.
       - Offering or accepting bribes or illegal gratification.

2.2 NOTICE AND CURE PROCEDURE
    a) Notice of Default:
       - Employer issues a written Notice of Default specifying:
         • Nature of default
         • Contract provisions breached
         • Required corrective actions
         • Proposed timeline to cure (usually 7–28 days depending on severity)

    b) Opportunity to Cure:
       - Contractor shall submit a written corrective action plan within the
         stipulated period and implement remedial measures.

    c) Failure to Remedy:
       - If Contractor fails to remedy the default within the cure period, or
         fails to demonstrate credible progress, Employer may:
         • Extend cure period once, or
         • Issue Notice of Termination.

2.3 NOTICE OF TERMINATION FOR DEFAULT
    a) Form:
       - Written notice on Employer’s letterhead, signed by authorised person.
    b) Content:
       - Reference to previous Notice(s) of Default.
       - Specific clause invoked.
       - Effective date of termination (immediate or future).
       - Scope (full or partial termination).

2.4 CONSEQUENCES OF TERMINATION FOR CONTRACTOR DEFAULT
    Upon termination for Contractor default:
    a) Suspension of Payments:
       - Further payments may be suspended except for certified work, subject
         to set-off for Employer’s losses.

    b) Taking Over of Site and Works:
       - Employer may take possession of:
         • Site and partially completed works.
         • Construction plant and equipment on site.
         • Materials, temporary works, and documents related to works.
       - Employer may continue works by:
         • Employing other contractors, or
         • Using own workforce/agents.

    c) Risk and Cost:
       - Additional cost incurred by Employer to complete the works shall be
         assessed and recovered from:
         • Unpaid amounts due to Contractor.
         • Retention money.
         • Performance Security / Bank Guarantees.
         • Separate recovery actions if shortfall remains.

    d) Forfeiture:
       - Employer may forfeit Performance Security as compensation for default,
         subject to legal limits and reasonableness.

    e) Exclusion of Loss of Profit:
       - Contractor shall generally not be entitled to loss of profit on
         unexecuted work when termination is due to its own default.

2.5 SURVIVAL OF OBLIGATIONS
    Contractor remains responsible for:
    - Defects in work executed prior to termination.
    - Indemnity obligations, IP rights, and confidentiality undertakings.
    - Compliance with safety and environmental obligations during demobilization.


SECTION 3: TERMINATION FOR EMPLOYER DEFAULT
================================================================================

3.1 GROUNDS FOR TERMINATION BY CONTRACTOR
    Contractor may, after giving notice, terminate the Contract if Employer:

    a) Non-Payment:
       - Fails to pay certified amounts within contractual period (e.g. 45–60 days)
         from due date, despite written reminder and additional cure period.

    b) Prolonged Suspension:
       - Suspends the works, in whole or substantial part, for reasons not
         attributable to Contractor for a continuous period (e.g. more than
         90 consecutive days).

    c) Failure to Provide Site / Approvals:
       - Does not provide access to Site, drawings, or approvals necessary for
         execution, despite repeated reminders.

    d) Material Breach:
       - Commits any substantial breach of its contractual obligations that
         materially affects Contractor’s ability to perform (e.g. failure to
         supply free-issue materials).

    e) Insolvency:
       - Becomes insolvent, is wound up, or enters into scheme of arrangement
         affecting its ability to perform.

3.2 NOTICE AND CURE PROCEDURE
    a) Contractor issues written notice specifying Employer’s default and
       provides a cure period (typically 28–60 days).
    b) If Employer fails to cure within this period, Contractor may issue a
       second notice terminating the Contract.

3.3 CONSEQUENCES OF TERMINATION FOR EMPLOYER DEFAULT
    Upon valid termination by Contractor:
    a) Payment for Work Done:
       - Contractor entitled to payment for:
         • Work executed up to date of termination.
         • Materials reasonably ordered and delivered to site or off-site
           storage (if title passes to Employer).
         • Demobilisation costs.

    b) Loss of Profit on Unexecuted Work:
       - Contractor may claim reasonable profit on the portion of works not
         executed due to Employer’s default, subject to proof and contract terms.

    c) Recovery of Costs:
       - Contractor entitled to:
         • Costs arising from idle resources due to Employer’s breach.
         • Reasonable overheads and site expenses.

    d) Release of Securities:
       - Performance Security / Retention Money to be released after adjustment
         of any legitimate Employer claims up to termination date.

    e) Removal of Equipment:
       - Contractor has right to remove its plant, tools, and unused materials,
         except where Employer elects to purchase them at agreed value.


SECTION 4: TERMINATION FOR CONVENIENCE
================================================================================

4.1 EMPLOYER’S RIGHT TO TERMINATE FOR CONVENIENCE
    Employer may, at its sole discretion and without Contractor default, terminate
    the Contract (in whole or in part) for convenience by giving written notice.

4.2 LIMITATIONS
    - Termination for convenience shall not be used in bad faith or to
      arbitrarily replace Contractor without justification.
    - Termination shall not be used to avoid payment of amounts already due.

4.3 CONTRACTOR’S ENTITLEMENTS ON TERMINATION FOR CONVENIENCE
    Upon such termination, Contractor shall be entitled to:

    a) Payment for:
       - Work executed up to date of termination.
       - Reasonable cost of materials ordered, manufactured, or in transit,
         to the extent Employer elects to take over title.
       - Demobilisation and site clearance costs.

    b) Reasonable Profit:
       - Reasonable profit on work done but not yet paid.
       - No loss of profit on unexecuted portion unless expressly provided.

    c) No Penalties:
       - No liquidated damages or penalties shall be applied for the terminated
         portion of the work.

4.4 PROCEDURE AFTER NOTICE
    a) Contractor to immediately stop the terminated portion of works.
    b) Secure and protect the Site and partially completed work.
    c) Submit termination account with:
       - Measurements up to date.
       - Inventory of materials and plant.
       - Demobilisation cost details.
    d) Employer to settle certified amount within agreed timeline.


SECTION 5: TERMINATION DUE TO FORCE MAJEURE
================================================================================

5.1 PROLONGED FORCE MAJEURE
    If Force Majeure events continue for a prolonged period (e.g. more than
    120–180 consecutive days) and substantially frustrate the purpose of the
    Contract, either Party may terminate by written notice.

5.2 CONSEQUENCES
    a) No fault termination:
       - Neither Party treated as being in default for the Force Majeure period.
    b) Payments:
       - Contractor paid for:
         • Work executed prior to Force Majeure.
         • Reasonable demobilisation costs.
       - No loss of profit for unexecuted work.
    c) Risk Allocation:
       - Parties bear their own consequential losses unless otherwise provided.


SECTION 6: MUTUAL TERMINATION
================================================================================

6.1 MUTUAL AGREEMENT
    The Parties may mutually agree to terminate the Contract at any time by
    executing a written termination agreement, specifying:

    - Effective date of termination.
    - Settlement of all outstanding payments and claims.
    - Distribution of materials, plant, and equipment.
    - Resolution of ongoing disputes (if any).

6.2 WAIVER OF FURTHER CLAIMS
    The Parties may agree that upon mutual termination and payment of agreed
    settlement sum, neither Party shall pursue further claims, except for:
    - Latent defects discovered later.
    - Fraud, gross negligence, or wilful misconduct.


SECTION 7: TERMINATION PROCEDURE AND HANDOVER
================================================================================

7.1 HANDOVER OBLIGATIONS
    Upon any termination:
    a) Contractor shall:
       - Stop work safely and progressively.
       - Protect partially completed works from damage.
       - Hand over:
         • Site and access.
         • As-built data and drawings for work executed.
         • Test records, quality documents, and O&M manuals (as applicable).

    b) Employer shall:
       - Provide reasonable access for demobilisation.
       - Confirm inventory of assets taken over.

7.2 DEMOBILISATION
    - Contractor shall demobilise manpower, plant, and temporary facilities in
      an orderly and safe manner within a mutually agreed timeframe.
    - Site shall be left in a clean and safe condition.

7.3 FINAL ACCOUNT
    a) Contractor to submit final account within specified period (e.g. 60 days)
       from termination effective date.
    b) Employer to review and certify within a reasonable period (e.g. 90 days).
    c) Any dispute on final account to be processed through dispute resolution
       mechanisms under the Contract.


SECTION 8: PARTIAL TERMINATION
================================================================================

8.1 SCOPE
    Employer may partially terminate certain portions, sections, or work items
    while retaining the remainder of the Contract.

8.2 EFFECT
    - Contract Price and Time for Completion shall be adjusted accordingly.
    - LD and performance obligations shall apply only to remaining scope.
    - Contractor shall be paid for terminated portion as per similar principles
      of termination for convenience, unless due to Contractor default.

8.3 SUBSTITUTION OF WORK
    - Employer may get the terminated portion executed through others, without
      prejudice to its right to recover additional cost in case of Contractor
      default.


SECTION 9: SURVIVING CLAUSES
================================================================================

9.1 CLAUSES THAT SURVIVE TERMINATION
    The following obligations shall survive termination, regardless of reason:
    - Intellectual Property and licence rights (to the extent granted/required).
    - Confidentiality and non-disclosure obligations.
    - Indemnity provisions.
    - Dispute resolution and arbitration clauses.
    - Governing law and jurisdiction clauses.
    - Defect liability and warranty obligations for work already completed
      (except where contract states otherwise).


SECTION 10: DISPUTE RESOLUTION ON TERMINATION
================================================================================

10.1 DISPUTED TERMINATION
    Where validity of termination is disputed:
    - Parties shall first attempt amicable settlement/negotiation.
    - If unresolved, dispute shall be referred to:
      • Conciliation / Adjudication, and failing that
      • Arbitration or court proceedings, as per dispute resolution clause.

10.2 CONTINUATION OF ESSENTIAL OBLIGATIONS
    During dispute:
    - Contractor shall, where instructed and paid, perform such essential
      safety and protection measures as required to prevent damage or loss.
    - Employer shall not unreasonably withhold payments due for undisputed
      amounts.

10.3 DAMAGES FOR WRONGFUL TERMINATION
    If it is subsequently determined that:
    - Employer wrongfully terminated for Contractor default, Contractor may be
      entitled to compensation similar to termination for Employer default.
    - Contractor wrongfully walked off and treated Employer as in default
      without valid grounds, Employer may be entitled to damages.
