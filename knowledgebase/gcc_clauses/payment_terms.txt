SECTION 1: CONTRACT PRICE AND PAYMENT STRUCTURE
================================================================================

1.1 CONTRACT SUM
    The total Contract Sum shall be as stated in the Contract Agreement and
    shall constitute full compensation to the Contractor for all works, services,
    materials, labor, equipment, taxes (except as otherwise provided), insurance,
    overhead, profit, and all other requirements necessary for the complete
    execution and completion of the project in accordance with the Contract
    Documents.

1.2 PRICE BASIS
    a) Lump Sum Contract: Fixed price for defined scope, subject only to approved
       variations and escalations as per contract terms

    b) Unit Rate Contract: Payment based on actual measured quantities at agreed
       unit rates as per Schedule of Rates (SOR)

    c) Cost Plus Fee Contract: Reimbursement of actual allowable costs plus an
       agreed fixed fee or percentage (typically 8-15% for overhead and profit)

    d) Hybrid/Mixed Contract: Combination of lump sum and unit rate methods for
       different work packages

1.3 PRICE ADJUSTMENT & ESCALATION
    Unless otherwise stated as "Firm Price Contract", the Contract Sum shall be
    subject to price adjustment under the following circumstances:

    a) VARIATION ORDERS: As issued by the Employer/Engineer with rate analysis

    b) STATUTORY CHANGES: Changes in GST, customs duty, or other statutory levies
       after the date of tender submission

    c) PRICE ESCALATION (if applicable):
       - Formula-based escalation using approved price indices (WPI/CPI)
       - Escalation applicable after 6 months from contract award
       - Maximum cap: 10% of original contract value
       - Base date for calculation: 28 days prior to bid submission
       - Index sources: Ministry of Statistics & Programme Implementation

    d) FORCE MAJEURE EVENTS: Subject to mutual agreement and supporting evidence


SECTION 2: PAYMENT MILESTONES AND SCHEDULE
================================================================================

2.1 ADVANCE PAYMENT (MOBILIZATION ADVANCE)
    a) Eligibility: Available for contracts exceeding Rs. 50 lakhs

    b) Amount: 
       - Standard: 10% of Contract Sum
       - For remote/difficult locations: Up to 15% (subject to approval)
       - Maximum ceiling: Rs. 5 crores (whichever is lower)

    c) Security Requirement:
       - Irrevocable Bank Guarantee from Scheduled Bank for 110% of advance
       - Validity: Until full recovery + 3 months
       - Format: As per Employer's standard format

    d) Purpose: Site mobilization, temporary facilities, initial material procurement

    e) Recovery Mechanism:
       - Proportionate recovery at 20% of each Running Account Bill
       - Recovery commences from 2nd bill onwards
       - Complete recovery before 80% project completion

    f) Payment Timeline: Within 14 working days of:
       - Submission of valid Bank Guarantee
       - Signing of Contract Agreement
       - Submission of advance payment request with justification

2.2 PROGRESS PAYMENTS (RUNNING ACCOUNT BILLS - RAB)
    a) Billing Frequency: Monthly (calendar month basis)

    b) Submission Timeline: On or before 7th day of succeeding month

    c) Minimum Billing Threshold:
       - Rs. 1,00,000 or 75% of previous certified payment (whichever is higher)
       - Final bill: No minimum threshold

    d) Measurement Basis:
       - Actual work executed and measured on site
       - As per standard method of measurement (IS codes/PWD/CPWD norms)
       - Joint measurement by Engineer and Contractor
       - Certified by authorized Engineer-in-Charge

    e) Documentation Required (Mandatory Attachments):
       - Measurement Book (MB) entries with sketches
       - Material reconciliation and consumption statement
       - Labour attendance and wage register
       - Quality test certificates (concrete, steel, soil, etc.)
       - Progress photographs with date stamps
       - Compliance certificates (safety, environmental)
       - Vendor invoices for major materials
       - Equipment deployment records
       - Insurance policy copies (if applicable)

    f) Payment Calculation:
       Gross Amount Billed                           Rs. X,XX,XX,XXX
       Less: Retention Money @ 5-10%                (Rs. XX,XX,XXX)
       Less: Advance Recovery @ 20%                 (Rs. XX,XX,XXX)
       Less: TDS @ 2% (as per Income Tax Act)       (Rs. XX,XXX)
       Less: GST TDS @ 2% (if applicable)           (Rs. XX,XXX)
       Less: Other recoveries (if any)              (Rs. XX,XXX)
       ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
       Net Payable Amount                           Rs. X,XX,XX,XXX

2.3 MILESTONE-BASED PAYMENTS (For Turnkey/EPC Projects)

    TYPICAL MILESTONE STRUCTURE FOR BUILDING CONSTRUCTION:
    ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
    Milestone No. | Description                  | % Payment | Cumulative
    ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
    M0           | Mobilization Advance          | 10%      | 10%
    M1           | Site Setup & Mobilization     | 5%       | 15%
    M2           | Foundation & Plinth Level     | 20%      | 35%
    M3           | Superstructure to 50%         | 15%      | 50%
    M4           | Superstructure Complete       | 15%      | 65%
    M5           | Roofing & Waterproofing       | 10%      | 75%
    M6           | Internal Finishes (80%)       | 10%      | 85%
    M7           | External Works & Services     | 5%       | 90%
    M8           | Testing & Commissioning       | 3%       | 93%
    M9           | Provisional Acceptance        | 5%       | 98%
    M10          | Final Payment (Post-warranty) | 2%       | 100%

    MILESTONE PAYMENT CONDITIONS:
    - Payment triggered only after milestone completion certificate from Engineer
    - Physical verification and quality compliance mandatory
    - Photographic evidence with timestamped images required
    - No payment for partially completed milestones
    - Disputed milestones: Undisputed portion released within timeline

2.4 MATERIAL ON SITE (MOS) PAYMENT
    a) Eligibility: Materials delivered to site but not yet incorporated in work

    b) Payment Percentage: Up to 75% of material value

    c) Conditions:
       - Materials conform to specifications
       - Delivered to designated storage area within project site
       - Properly stored and protected from damage
       - Invoices and delivery challans submitted
       - Insurance coverage in place
       - Materials remain property of Employer until incorporated
       - Not applicable for perishable or consumable items

    d) Exclusions:
       - Tools, equipment, and machinery
       - Materials not specifically required for the works
       - Materials at off-site locations (unless specifically agreed)


SECTION 3: PAYMENT PROCESSING AND TIMELINES
================================================================================

3.1 SUBMISSION OF BILLS
    The Contractor shall submit bills through the following process:

    a) Format: As per prescribed standard format (Appendix-A)

    b) Copies: 3 hard copies + 1 soft copy (Excel/PDF)

    c) Submission to: Engineer-in-Charge/Project Manager

    d) Timeline: Within 7 days of close of billing period

    e) Late Submission: Bills submitted after deadline may be processed in
       next billing cycle

3.2 VERIFICATION, MEASUREMENT AND CERTIFICATION
    a) Engineer's Verification: Within 14 days of bill submission
       - Site verification and measurement check
       - Documentation review and compliance verification
       - Quality test certificate validation

    b) Queries/Discrepancies: Notified to Contractor within 7 days
       - Contractor to respond within 3 days
       - Revised bill submission within 5 days

    c) Certification by Engineer: Within 7 days after verification
       - Interim Payment Certificate (IPC) issued
       - Certified amount may differ from claimed amount
       - Reasons for variations to be documented

    d) Employer's Approval: Within 3 days of certification
       - Final approval for payment processing
       - Payment recommendation forwarded to Accounts

3.3 PAYMENT TIMELINE (CRITICAL TIMELINES)

    a) STANDARD PAYMENT CYCLE:
       Day 0:     Contractor submits bill
       Day 1-14:  Engineer verification and measurement
       Day 15-21: Certification and approval
       Day 22-28: Payment processing and release
       ────────────────────────────────────────────
       Total:     28 days from bill submission (Net 28)

    b) GOVERNMENT PROJECTS (as per MSME Act, 2006):
       - Payment within 30 days for MSME contractors
       - Interest @ 3% above Bank Rate for delayed payments beyond 30 days
       - Payment within 45 days for non-MSME contractors (as per GFR)

    c) PRIVATE SECTOR PROJECTS:
       - Negotiable terms: Net 21, Net 30, Net 45, or Net 60
       - Industry standard: Net 30-45 days
       - Early payment discount: 2% if paid within 10 days (2/10 Net 30)

    d) DISPUTED AMOUNTS:
       - Undisputed portion must be paid within contractual timeline
       - Disputed portion held pending resolution
       - Interest applicable on delayed undisputed payments

3.4 MODE OF PAYMENT
    All payments shall be made through electronic mode only:

    a) Preferred Modes:
       - NEFT (National Electronic Funds Transfer)
       - RTGS (Real Time Gross Settlement) - for amounts > Rs. 2 lakhs
       - IMPS (Immediate Payment Service) - for urgent small payments

    b) Payment Advice:
       - Detailed payment advice with bill reference sent via email
       - Breakup of gross amount, deductions, and net payment
       - TDS certificate (Form 16A) issued quarterly

    c) Bank Account Requirements:
       - Designated bank account in Contractor's name
       - PAN-linked account mandatory
       - IFSC code and branch details verified

    d) Cheque Payments (Exceptions only):
       - Allowed only for petty payments below Rs. 25,000
       - Subject to prior approval from Accounts Department


SECTION 4: RETENTION MONEY
================================================================================

4.1 RETENTION PERCENTAGE
    a) Standard Rate: 5% of each Running Account Bill

    b) Higher Retention: 10% for complex/critical works (MEP, structural steel)

    c) Maximum Retention Limit:
       - 10% of Total Contract Sum, or
       - 5% if Retention < Rs. 25 lakhs

    d) Calculation Example:
       Gross Bill Amount:              Rs. 50,00,000
       Retention @ 5%:                 Rs. 2,50,000
       Cumulative Retention so far:    Rs. 12,00,000
       Maximum Retention (10% of 
       Rs. 2 crore contract):          Rs. 20,00,000
       Status: Retention continues as limit not reached

4.2 PURPOSE OF RETENTION
    - Security against defective work and non-performance
    - Cover for rectification of defects during Defect Liability Period (DLP)
    - Ensure contractor's continued engagement post-completion

4.3 RELEASE OF RETENTION MONEY

    a) FIRST RELEASE (50% of Retention):
       - Trigger: Issuance of Taking Over Certificate (TOC)
       - Timeline: Within 30 days of TOC
       - Condition: No major defects or non-compliances

    b) SECOND RELEASE (Remaining 50%):
       - Trigger: Completion of Defect Liability Period
       - Standard DLP: 12 months from Taking Over
       - Extended DLP: 24 months for MEP/specialized works
       - Timeline: Within 30 days of DLP completion certificate
       - Condition: All defects rectified satisfactorily

    c) SUBSTITUTION OF RETENTION:
       - Bank Guarantee in lieu of cash retention (if agreed)
       - Amount: 110% of retention amount
       - Validity: Until DLP completion + 3 months
       - Benefit: Improves contractor's cash flow

4.4 INTEREST ON RETENTION (If Applicable)
    - Some contracts provide interest on retention @ 4-6% p.a.
    - Paid along with retention release
    - Not applicable in most government contracts


SECTION 5: DEDUCTIONS AND RECOVERIES
================================================================================

5.1 STATUTORY DEDUCTIONS

    a) TAX DEDUCTED AT SOURCE (TDS):
       - Rate: 2% on contract payments (Section 194C, Income Tax Act)
       - Applicable: On gross bill amount before other deductions
       - TDS Certificate: Form 16A issued quarterly
       - PAN mandatory for TDS credit

    b) GST TDS (if applicable):
       - Rate: 2% on contract value > Rs. 2.5 lakhs (some states)
       - Varies by state GST rules
       - Credit available to contractor in GST returns

    c) LABOUR CESS:
       - Building & Other Construction Workers Welfare Cess @ 1%
       - Calculated on construction cost
       - Deposited with Labour Welfare Board

5.2 CONTRACTUAL DEDUCTIONS

    a) Advance Recovery: As per agreed percentage (typically 20% of each bill)

    b) Retention Money: 5-10% as per contract terms

    c) Equipment/Materials Hire: If contractor uses Employer's equipment

    d) Electricity & Water Charges: Actual consumption charges

    e) Security/Labour Accommodation: If provided by Employer

5.3 LIQUIDATED DAMAGES (LD)
    a) For Delay in Completion:
       - Typical Rate: 0.5% to 1% per week of delay
       - Maximum Cap: 10% of Contract Sum
       - Calculated on delayed portion/milestone value

    b) For Performance Shortfall:
       - As per agreed KPIs and penalty matrix
       - Deducted from Running Account Bills

    c) Conditions for Waiver:
       - Force Majeure events
       - Delay due to Employer's acts (extension of time granted)
       - Timely submission of Extension of Time (EOT) claim

5.4 BACK CHARGES & SET-OFF
    The Employer reserves the right to recover:
    - Cost of rectification of defective work done by others
    - Cost of completing abandoned work
    - Insurance premium if contractor fails to insure
    - Demurrage charges for delayed site clearance
    - Environmental penalties due to contractor's acts


SECTION 6: FINAL PAYMENT AND ACCOUNT SETTLEMENT
================================================================================

6.1 FINAL BILL SUBMISSION
    a) Timing: Within 60 days of Taking Over Certificate

    b) Components:
       - Final measurement of all executed works
       - All approved variations and change orders
       - Claims for unforeseen ground conditions (if any)
       - Reconciliation of advance, retention, deductions

    c) Documentation:
       - As-built drawings
       - Operation & Maintenance manuals
       - Warranty certificates
       - Test certificates and compliance reports
       - No Objection Certificate (NOC) from sub-contractors
       - All statutory clearances

6.2 FINAL ACCOUNT PREPARATION
    a) Timeline: Engineer to finalize within 90 days of final bill submission

    b) Components:
       CREDIT SIDE:
       - Total certified value of work executed
       - Approved variations and extra items
       - Escalation (if applicable)

       DEBIT SIDE:
       - All payments made (RABs + advance)
       - Retention money held
       - Liquidated damages
       - All recoveries and deductions

       NET PAYABLE: Credit - Debit = Final Settlement Amount

6.3 FINAL PAYMENT RELEASE
    a) 90% Payment: Within 30 days of final account agreement

    b) 10% Payment: Released after Defect Liability Period completion
       - OR substituted by Performance Bank Guarantee

    c) Conditions Precedent:
       - Mutual acceptance of final account
       - Submission of No-Claim Certificate (if required)
       - Clearance of all site obligations
       - Handover of O&M manuals and as-built drawings

6.4 DISPUTE RESOLUTION
    In case of disagreement on final account:
    - Undisputed amount released within 30 days
    - Disputed items referred to dispute resolution mechanism
    - Options: Conciliation → Arbitration → Court (as per contract)


SECTION 7: SPECIAL PAYMENT PROVISIONS
================================================================================

7.1 INTERIM RATE FOR NEW ITEMS
    For work items not in Schedule of Rates:
    a) Contractor submits rate analysis within 7 days
    b) Engineer approves interim rate within 14 days (90% of estimated final rate)
    c) Payment made at interim rate
    d) Final rate negotiated and difference adjusted in subsequent bills

7.2 PAYMENT FOR SUSPENDED WORK
    If work suspended due to Employer's reasons:
    a) Contractor entitled to payment for work done till suspension date
    b) Standing charges for idle equipment/labour (if justified)
    c) Demobilization and re-mobilization costs (if suspension > 60 days)

7.3 PAYMENT ON TERMINATION
    a) Termination by Employer (Contractor default):
       - Payment for satisfactory work completed
       - Less all damages and completion costs
       - Retention and advance adjustment

    b) Termination by Contractor (Employer default):
       - Payment for all work completed
       - Reasonable profit on incomplete work
       - Costs of materials procured
       - Demobilization costs

    c) Termination for Convenience:
       - Payment for work completed + reasonable profit
       - Costs of materials ordered
       - Demobilization costs

7.4 PAYMENT IN FOREIGN CURRENCY (for International Contracts)
    a) Split between local and foreign currency as per contract
    b) Exchange rate: As on 28 days before bid submission
    c) Payment in foreign currency through SWIFT/wire transfer
    d) Compliance with FEMA regulations and RBI guidelines


SECTION 8: CLAIMS AND VARIATIONS
================================================================================

8.1 VARIATION ORDERS
    a) Employer's right to vary the scope within ±25% of contract value

    b) Rate Determination:
       - Rates in Schedule of Rates (if applicable)
       - Derived rates based on similar items
       - Rate analysis and negotiation for new items

    c) Payment: Included in Running Account Bills after approval

8.2 CLAIMS PROCEDURE
    a) Notice of Claim: Within 28 days of claim-giving event

    b) Detailed Claim Submission: Within 60 days with full justification

    c) Types of Claims:
       - Extension of Time (EOT) with cost implications
       - Unforeseen ground conditions
       - Delay damages due to Employer's acts
       - Price escalation beyond contractual limits

    d) Claim Assessment: Within 90 days by Engineer

    e) Payment: After mutual agreement or dispute resolution

SECTION 11: COMPLIANCE AND DOCUMENTATION
================================================================================

11.1 MANDATORY COMPLIANCES FOR PAYMENT
     - GST registration and monthly GST returns filed
     - PF & ESI registration (if employee strength > threshold)
     - Monthly PF & ESI challans deposited
     - Labour license and compliance registers
     - Valid insurance policies (Workmen Compensation, CAR policy)
     - Environmental clearances (if required)
     - Safety compliance certificates

11.2 PAYMENT WITHHELD UNTIL:
     - Submission of statutory compliance certificates
     - Resolution of major non-compliances or defects
     - Rectification of previously rejected work
     - Settlement of disputes with sub-contractors
     - Clearance of site encumbrances


SECTION 12: INTEREST ON DELAYED PAYMENTS
================================================================================

12.1 APPLICABILITY
     If payment not made within contractual timeline due to Employer's fault:

     a) Interest Rate:
        - Government Contracts: 3% above Bank Rate (RBI)
        - Private Contracts: As agreed (typically 12-18% p.a.)
        - MSME Contractors: Compound interest @ 3x Bank Rate

     b) Calculation Period:
        - From due date to actual payment date
        - Simple interest for government contracts
        - Compound interest for MSME (as per MSME Act)

     c) Exclusions:
        - Delays due to contractor's fault (incomplete documents)
        - Disputed amounts under arbitration
        - Force Majeure events

12.2 CLAIM PROCEDURE
     - Written notice within 30 days of payment delay
     - Interest claim submitted with next bill
     - Subject to approval by Engineer/Employer


SECTION 13: PAYMENT SECURITY INSTRUMENTS
================================================================================

13.1 BANK GUARANTEES
     a) Mobilization Advance: 110% of advance, valid till full recovery + 3 months
     b) Performance Guarantee: 5-10% of contract value, valid till DLP + 3 months
     c) Retention Guarantee: 110% of retention, valid till DLP completion + 3 months

     Format: Unconditional, irrevocable, payable on demand

13.2 LETTERS OF CREDIT (for international contracts)
     - Confirmed, irrevocable LC from reputed bank
     - Validity: Until final payment + 60 days
     - Partial shipments and payments allowed

13.3 ESCROW ACCOUNTS (for large projects)
     - Joint escrow account for fund management
     - Controlled release as per payment milestones
     - Provides payment security to contractor
