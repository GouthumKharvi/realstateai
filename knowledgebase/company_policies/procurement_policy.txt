SECTION 1: OBJECTIVES AND PRINCIPLES
================================================================================

1.1 OBJECTIVES
    The procurement function exists to:

    a) VALUE CREATION:
       - Ensure timely, efficient, and cost-effective procurement.
       - Optimize total cost of ownership (TCO), not just purchase price.
       - Achieve value for money while maintaining quality and compliance.

    b) RISK MANAGEMENT:
       - Diversify supply base to reduce dependency risks.
       - Ensure business continuity through strategic sourcing.
       - Mitigate quality, delivery, and financial risks.

    c) COMPLIANCE AND GOVERNANCE:
       - Comply with applicable legal, tax, ethical, and regulatory requirements.
       - Maintain audit trail and documentation for accountability.
       - Support internal controls and prevent fraud, waste, and abuse.

    d) STAKEHOLDER SATISFACTION:
       - Meet internal customer requirements (departments/projects).
       - Build fair, transparent, and sustainable vendor relationships.
       - Support organizational goals (sustainability, local sourcing, MSME).

1.2 CORE PRINCIPLES

    a) TRANSPARENCY:
       - Clear procurement procedures published and accessible.
       - Documented decision-making with rationale.
       - Open communication of requirements and evaluation criteria.

    b) FAIRNESS AND NON-DISCRIMINATION:
       - Equal treatment of all qualified vendors regardless of size, location.
       - No favoritism or arbitrary exclusions.
       - Objective evaluation based on pre-announced criteria.

    c) COMPETITION:
       - Maximum feasible competition to ensure best value.
       - Wide market reach for major procurements.
       - Avoidance of cartels, bid-rigging, and collusion.

    d) ACCOUNTABILITY:
       - Defined approval authorities (Delegation of Authority - DoA).
       - Personal responsibility for procurement decisions.
       - Consequences for policy violations.

    e) INTEGRITY AND ETHICS:
       - Zero tolerance for bribery, fraud, corruption, or conflict of interest.
       - Adherence to Code of Conduct for procurement personnel.
       - Protection of confidential and proprietary information.

    f) SUSTAINABILITY:
       - Consideration of environmental, social, and governance (ESG) factors.
       - Support for local suppliers, MSMEs, and inclusive growth.
       - Life-cycle thinking (energy efficiency, recyclability, durability).

2.3 PROCUREMENT METHODS - DETAILED

    A. PETTY/SPOT PURCHASE (Band A):
       - Use: Urgent, low-value, one-time needs.
       - Process:
         • Requestor identifies need and obtains verbal/written quote.
         • If above Rs. 25,000, minimum one written quotation required.
         • Approval as per DoA.
         • Direct purchase through petty cash or designated account.
       - Documentation: PR, quotation, invoice, payment voucher.

    B. LIMITED ENQUIRY/QUOTATION (Band B):
       - Use: Moderate value, known suppliers, time constraints.
       - Process:
         • Minimum 3 quotations from approved/empanelled vendors.
         • RFQ with clear specifications, delivery, payment terms.
         • Comparative statement prepared showing price, terms, quality.
         • Selection of L1 or justified alternative.
         • Approval as per DoA.
       - Timeline: 5-7 working days for quotation submission.

    C. COMPETITIVE RFQ/OPEN TENDER (Band C):
       - Use: High-value goods and services, multiple qualified vendors.
       - Process:
         • Preparation of detailed RFQ/Tender document.
         • Publication on company portal/e-procurement platform.
         • Pre-bid meeting (if required) for clarifications.
         • Two-envelope system: Technical + Commercial.
         • Technical evaluation by cross-functional team.
         • Commercial opening only for technically qualified bidders.
         • Detailed evaluation report with recommendation.
         • Approval as per DoA.
       - Timeline: 15-30 days depending on complexity.

    D. FORMAL OPEN TENDER (Band D):
       - Use: Major projects, high-value procurement, public accountability.
       - Process:
         • Detailed tender documentation (GCC, SCC, BOQ, drawings).
         • Wide publicity through newspapers, websites, industry portals.
         • Pre-qualification (PQ) stage may precede technical bidding.
         • Mandatory site visit and pre-bid conference.
         • Multi-stage evaluation (PQ → Technical → Commercial).
         • Tender Evaluation Committee (TEC) review.
         • Board/senior management approval.
         • Negotiation (if permitted by policy).
         • Award and contract signing.
       - Timeline: 45-90 days.

    E. SINGLE/SOLE SOURCE PROCUREMENT:
       - Allowed ONLY for:
         • Proprietary items (OEM certification required).
         • Standardization requirements (existing installed base).
         • Emergency situations (life/safety/critical operations).
         • Specialized consultants (unique expertise).
       - Requirements:
         • Written justification by requestor.
         • Market research to confirm no alternatives.
         • Price reasonableness certification.
         • Higher authority approval (minimum one level above normal DoA).

    F. RATE CONTRACT/FRAMEWORK AGREEMENT:
       - Use: Repetitive purchases of similar items over a period.
       - Process:
         • Competitive bidding for rates valid for 1-3 years.
         • Quantities estimated but not committed.
         • Call-off orders issued as needed against agreed rates.
         • Annual price revision clauses (WPI/CPI linked).
       - Benefits: Reduced transaction costs, assured supply, price stability.

    G. E-AUCTION/REVERSE AUCTION:
       - Use: Commodities, standard goods with clear specifications.
       - Process:
         • Online platform where vendors bid down prices in real-time.
         • Pre-qualified vendors participate.
         • L1 bidder wins subject to post-auction verification.
       - Benefits: Transparency, dynamic pricing, time efficiency.

    H. TWO-STAGE BIDDING:
       - Use: Complex projects where scope needs refinement.
       - Stage 1: Technical proposals without price.
       - Negotiations and scope finalization.
       - Stage 2: Financial bids from shortlisted vendors.


SECTION 3: PROCUREMENT PROCESS - STEP-BY-STEP
================================================================================

3.1 STAGE 1: INDENT/PURCHASE REQUISITION (PR)

    A. INITIATION:
       - User department identifies requirement.
       - Checks budget availability in approved budget/project estimate.
       - Raises PR in ERP/Procurement system with:
         • Item description, quantity, specification.
         • Justification and business case (for high-value).
         • Required delivery date and location.
         • Cost center/project code.
         • Suggested vendors (if any).

    B. SPECIFICATIONS:
       - Must be:
         • Clear, complete, and unambiguous.
         • Performance-based (not brand-biased) unless justified.
         • Include quality standards, certifications (IS/ISO/BIS).
       - For works: Scope of work, drawings, BOQ, method statements.

    C. APPROVAL OF PR:
       - Department head reviews and approves.
       - Budget controller verifies budget availability.
       - Procurement team validates specification and feasibility.

3.2 STAGE 2: SOURCING STRATEGY

    A. MARKET RESEARCH:
       - Identify potential vendors (existing, new, global).
       - Assess market conditions (availability, pricing trends).
       - Check empanelled vendor list and past performance.

    B. PROCUREMENT METHOD SELECTION:
       - Based on value band, category, urgency, and market dynamics.
       - Document rationale if deviation from standard method.

    C. VENDOR SHORTLISTING (for limited enquiry):
       - Minimum criteria: Technical capability, financial strength, compliance.
       - Diversity consideration: New vendors, MSMEs, local suppliers.

3.3 STAGE 3: RFQ/TENDER PREPARATION

    A. DOCUMENT COMPONENTS:
       1. Instructions to Bidders (ITB)
       2. General Conditions of Contract (GCC)
       3. Special Conditions of Contract (SCC)
       4. Technical Specifications / Scope of Work
       5. Bill of Quantities (BOQ) / Schedule of Rates
       6. Drawings and Annexures
       7. Standard Forms (Bid Form, BG formats, Compliance Matrix)

    B. EVALUATION CRITERIA:
       - Must be defined BEFORE tender issue.
       - Technical Parameters:
         • Compliance with specifications (Pass/Fail or scoring).
         • Experience and track record (years, similar projects).
         • Technical capacity (equipment, manpower, certifications).
         • Quality systems (ISO 9001, product certifications).
         • HSE record (for works contractors).
       - Commercial Parameters:
         • Total evaluated cost (base price + lifecycle cost + taxes + delivery).
         • Payment terms (L/C, advance requirements).
         • Delivery schedule (penalties for delay).
         • Warranty and post-sales support.

    C. TIMELINES:
       - Bid preparation period: Minimum 7-21 days depending on complexity.
       - Bid validity: Typically 90-180 days.
       - Evaluation and award: Within bid validity period.

3.4 STAGE 4: BID INVITATION AND MANAGEMENT

    A. PUBLICATION:
       - Band C & D: Company website, e-procurement portal.
       - Band D: Additional newspaper advertisement (for public projects).
       - Direct email to shortlisted/empanelled vendors.

    B. PRE-BID MEETING:
       - For complex/high-value tenders.
       - Site visit (mandatory for works contracts).
       - Clarifications recorded in minutes and issued as addendum.
       - Amendments to tender document (if required).

    C. BID SUBMISSION:
       - Online submission preferred (e-procurement).
       - Two-envelope system: Technical and Commercial in separate sealed envelopes.
       - Late bids rejected (no exceptions).
       - Bids logged with date-time stamp.

3.5 STAGE 5: BID OPENING

    A. TECHNICAL BID OPENING:
       - Conducted by Tender Opening Committee (minimum 2-3 members).
       - Date and time pre-announced in tender.
       - Public opening (for transparency) or recorded opening.
       - Attendance register maintained.
       - Each bid checked for:
         • EMD/Bid Security.
         • Completeness of documents.
         • Signatures and authorization.
       - Minutes prepared and signed by committee.

    B. COMMERCIAL BID OPENING:
       - ONLY for bidders who pass technical evaluation.
       - Conducted after technical evaluation is complete and approved.
       - Same transparency and documentation as technical opening.

3.6 STAGE 6: EVALUATION

    A. TECHNICAL EVALUATION:
       - Evaluation Team: Cross-functional (Technical, Procurement, QA, HSE).
       - Compliance Matrix:
         ┌────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
         │ Parameter          │ Requirement │ Bidder A │ Bidder B │ Remarks│
         ├────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
         │ Experience (years) │ Min 5       │ 8        │ 6        │ Pass   │
         │ Turnover (Cr)      │ Min 10      │ 15       │ 12       │ Pass   │
         │ ISO 9001 Cert      │ Mandatory   │ Yes      │ Yes      │ Pass   │
         │ Similar projects   │ Min 3       │ 5        │ 2        │ Fail   │
         └────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
       - Scoring System (if applicable):
         • Each parameter assigned weightage.
         • Minimum qualifying score: e.g., 70/100.
       - Site/factory visit (if critical).
       - Reference checks (past clients).

    B. COMMERCIAL EVALUATION:
       - Price Comparison:
         ┌───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
         │ Description    │ Unit│ Qty │ Bidder A │ Bidder B │ Bidder C │
         ├───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
         │ Item 1         │ No  │ 100 │ 500      │ 480      │ 510      │
         │ Item 2         │ No  │ 50  │ 1200     │ 1250     │ 1180     │
         │ Subtotal       │     │     │ 110,000  │ 110,500  │ 110,000  │
         │ GST @ 18%      │     │     │ 19,800   │ 19,890   │ 19,800   │
         │ Freight        │     │     │ 5,000    │ Included │ 6,000    │
         │ Total Landed   │     │     │ 134,800  │ 130,390  │ 135,800  │
         │ Rank           │     │     │ L2       │ L1       │ L3       │
         └───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
       - Payment Terms Impact:
         • Advance requirement (cash flow impact).
         • Credit period (NPV calculation if significant).
       - Warranty/AMC value (extended coverage).
       - Total Cost of Ownership (TCO):
         • Purchase price + installation + training + maintenance + energy
           consumption + disposal cost.

    C. EVALUATION REPORT:
       - Summary of bids received.
       - Technical evaluation matrix and scores.
       - Commercial comparison.
       - Recommendation with justification.
       - Deviation from L1 (if any) with reasons:
         • Better quality/warranty.
         • Faster delivery.
         • Total cost of ownership (TCO) analysis.
         • Strategic considerations (OEM, local content, MSME).
       - Risk assessment.

3.7 STAGE 7: NEGOTIATION (IF PERMITTED)

    - Allowed For:
      • Single source procurement.
      • Post-L1 selection for clarifications (not price negotiation in open tender).
      • Two-stage bidding.
    - Not Allowed:
      • Reverse auction after L1 identification (unethical).
      • Asking L1 to match lower price after evaluation (bid manipulation).

    - Documentation:
      • Minutes of negotiation meeting.
      • Final agreed terms and price.
      • Approval for negotiated price.

3.8 STAGE 8: APPROVAL AND AWARD

    A. APPROVAL LEVELS (Delegation of Authority - DoA):
       ┌────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
       │ Value (INR Lakhs) │ Approving Authority                   │
       ├────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
       │ Up to 1           │ Department Head                       │
       │ 1 to 5            │ BU Head / Project Manager             │
       │ 5 to 25           │ VP / General Manager                  │
       │ 25 to 100         │ CFO + BU Head (joint)                 │
       │ 100 to 500        │ CEO / MD                              │
       │ Above 500         │ Board of Directors / Procurement Committee│
       └────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

    B. AWARD DOCUMENTATION:
       - Letter of Award (LoA) / Letter of Intent (LoI).
       - Purchase Order (PO) / Work Order (WO).
       - Contract Agreement (for high-value/complex).

    C. REGRET LETTERS:
       - Issued to unsuccessful bidders.
       - Brief reason for non-selection (optional but good practice).
       - Thanks for participation and invitation for future opportunities.

3.9 STAGE 9: CONTRACT EXECUTION AND MANAGEMENT

    A. PRE-EXECUTION:
       - Performance security / Bank Guarantee submission.
       - Insurance certificates (CAR, WC, TPL).
       - Tax registrations (GST, PAN, TDS exemption if applicable).
       - Safety and site induction (for works).

    B. EXECUTION MONITORING:
       - Delivery tracking (goods).
       - Progress monitoring (works/services).
       - Quality inspections and testing.
       - Payment processing against milestones/deliveries.
       - Change order management.

    C. PERFORMANCE EVALUATION:
       - Vendor scorecard updated.
       - Issues and non-conformances recorded.
       - Lessons learned documented.

3.10 STAGE 10: RECEIPT, INSPECTION, AND PAYMENT

    A. GOODS RECEIPT:
       - Physical verification against PO (quantity, description).
       - Quality inspection (visual, testing as per specs).
       - Goods Receipt Note (GRN) generated in ERP.
       - Rejection note for non-conforming goods.

    B. WORKS COMPLETION:
       - Joint measurement with contractor.
       - Measurement Book (MB) entries.
       - Quality test certificates verified.
       - Taking Over Certificate (TOC) issued.

    C. PAYMENT PROCESSING:
       - Invoice received with:
         • Valid PO/WO reference.
         • GRN/MB number.
         • Tax invoice with GST details.
       - 3-way match: PO + GRN + Invoice.
       - Deductions: Advance recovery, retention, LD, TDS.
       - Payment approval as per DoA.
       - Payment within agreed timeline (Net 30/45 days).
       - MSME vendors: Payment within 45 days mandatory (MSME Act, 2006).


SECTION 4: SPECIAL PROCUREMENT SCENARIOS
================================================================================

4.1 EMERGENCY PROCUREMENT

    A. DEFINITION:
       - Unforeseen situation threatening life, safety, critical operations, or
         causing significant financial loss if not addressed immediately.

    B. ALLOWED DEVIATIONS:
       - Waiver of competitive bidding.
       - Single source selection.
       - Shortened approval timelines.

    C. CONDITIONS:
       - Written justification by requestor.
       - Certification by department head that emergency is genuine.
       - Approval by one level higher than normal DoA.
       - Post-facto review by procurement committee.
       - Measures to prevent recurrence (if due to poor planning).

    D. DOCUMENTATION:
       - Emergency procurement register maintained.
       - Auditable trail of decisions and approvals.

4.2 MSME PREFERENCE

    - As per MSME Development Act, 2006 and subsequent amendments:
      • Preference to MSME vendors in case of price parity (within 15% of L1).
      • Advance payment up to 20% without BG for MSMEs (if policy permits).
      • Prompt payment within 45 days (interest @ 3x Bank Rate for delay).

    - Purchase Preference:
      • Minimum 25% procurement from MSMEs (as per Government guidelines
        for PSUs and large corporates adopting similar norms).
      • 3% sub-target for MSMEs owned by SC/ST entrepreneurs (if applicable).

4.3 LOCAL CONTENT AND DOMESTIC PREFERENCE

    - For projects with Government funding or policy mandates:
      • "Make in India" preference.
      • Local content verification and certification.
      • Price preference margin (e.g., 20%) for domestic bidders.

    - Internal CSR Goals:
      • Support local suppliers to boost regional economy.
      • Reduce logistics cost and carbon footprint.

4.4 SUSTAINABILITY AND GREEN PROCUREMENT

    - Preference for:
      • Energy-efficient equipment (5-star rated, BEE certified).
      • Recyclable, biodegradable, or low-emission products.
      • Vendors with ISO 14001 (Environmental Management).
      • Responsible sourcing (conflict-free minerals, fair trade).

    - Life Cycle Cost Analysis:
      • Not just purchase price, but operating cost over useful life.

4.5 PROPRIETARY AND SINGLE SOURCE

    - Justification Required:
      • Technical monopoly (only one OEM/patent holder).
      • Compatibility with existing systems (standardization).
      • After-market support (spares, service network).

    - Approval Process:
      • OEM certificate or declaration of proprietary nature.
      • Market research report showing no alternatives.
      • Reasonableness of price (benchmarking, historical data).
      • Higher authority sign-off.

4.6 GLOBAL/IMPORT PROCUREMENT

    - Additional Considerations:
      • Incoterms (FOB, CIF, DAP, etc.).
      • Foreign exchange risk (hedging, payment terms).
      • Customs duty, import taxes, clearance time.
      • BIS/ISI certifications for imported goods.
      • Warranty and after-sales support in India.

    - Documentation:
      • Import license (if required).
      • Bill of lading, certificate of origin.
      • Pre-shipment inspection (for high-value).


SECTION 5: ETHICS, CONFLICT OF INTEREST, AND CODE OF CONDUCT
================================================================================

5.1 CODE OF CONDUCT FOR PROCUREMENT PERSONNEL

    A. INTEGRITY:
       - Act in the best interest of the Organization.
       - No solicitation or acceptance of gifts, favors, or kickbacks.
       - No personal gain from procurement decisions.

    B. PROFESSIONALISM:
       - Treat all vendors with respect and fairness.
       - Maintain confidentiality of bids and proprietary information.
       - Avoid even the appearance of impropriety.

    C. COMPETENCE:
       - Continuously upgrade knowledge and skills.
       - Seek expert advice when dealing with unfamiliar categories.
       - Use best practices and professional judgment.

5.2 CONFLICT OF INTEREST

    A. DISCLOSURE REQUIREMENTS:
       - Employees must disclose:
         • Financial interest in vendor (shareholding, directorship).
         • Family relationship with vendor personnel.
         • Personal relationship (close friendship, past employment).
       - Disclosure form to be submitted annually and on case-by-case basis.

    B. RECUSAL:
       - Employee with conflict must:
         • Abstain from decision-making.
         • Not participate in evaluation or negotiation.
         • Hand over to alternate procurement officer.

    C. CONSEQUENCES OF NON-DISCLOSURE:
       - Disciplinary action (warning to termination).
       - Legal action if fraud or corruption involved.

5.3 GIFTS AND HOSPITALITY

    - GENERAL RULE: No gifts or favors from vendors.

    - EXCEPTIONS (if policy permits):
      • Promotional items of nominal value (< Rs. 500).
      • Business meals during official meetings (reasonable cost).

    - ALWAYS PROHIBITED:
      • Cash or cash equivalent.
      • Gifts during active procurement process.
      • Expensive gifts, foreign trips, or entertainment.

    - DECLARATION:
      • All gifts (even allowed ones) must be declared to management.

5.4 ANTI-CORRUPTION AND BRIBERY

    - Zero Tolerance Policy:
      • No bribe, facilitation payment, or improper advantage.
      • Compliance with Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988.
      • Adherence to any applicable foreign anti-corruption laws (FCPA, UK
        Bribery Act) if Organization has international operations.

    - Whistleblower Protection:
      • Anonymous reporting channel for suspected violations.
      • Protection against retaliation for good-faith reporting.
      • Investigation and appropriate action.

5.5 VENDOR BLACKLISTING AND DEBARMENT

    - Grounds for Blacklisting:
      • Fraud, misrepresentation, or submission of forged documents.
      • Bribery or corrupt practices.
      • Persistent poor quality or non-performance.
      • Breach of contract or legal violations.
      • Involvement in cartel or bid-rigging.

    - Procedure:
      • Show-cause notice to vendor.
      • Opportunity to respond.
      • Review by senior management/committee.
      • Decision with debarment period (e.g., 1-5 years).
      • Communication to all business units and industry (where appropriate).


SECTION 6: DOCUMENTATION, RECORDS, AND AUDIT
================================================================================

6.1 MANDATORY DOCUMENTATION

    For Each Procurement:
    - Purchase Requisition (PR) with approvals.
    - RFQ/Tender document.
    - Bid opening minutes.
    - Evaluation report with approvals.
    - Comparative statement.
    - Approval note / Sanction order.
    - Purchase Order / Work Order / Contract Agreement.
    - Performance security and insurances.
    - GRN / Completion certificate.
    - Invoices and payment records.
    - Vendor performance evaluation.

6.2 RECORD RETENTION

    - Minimum Retention Period:
      • Procurement below Rs. 10 lakhs: 3 years.
      • Procurement Rs. 10 lakhs to 1 crore: 5 years.
      • Procurement above Rs. 1 crore: 7 years or as per legal requirement.
      • Litigation or dispute: Until final resolution + 2 years.

    - Format:
      • Physical files for contracts and critical documents.
      • Digital repository (ERP, DMS) for all supporting documents.
      • Backup and disaster recovery for digital records.

6.3 INTERNAL AUDIT

    - Scope:
      • Compliance with procurement policy.
      • Approval authorities and segregation of duties.
      • Competitive process adherence.
      • Proper documentation and record-keeping.
      • Vendor selection rationale.
      • Price reasonableness.

    - Frequency:
      • Annual audit of all procurements above Rs. 50 lakhs.
      • Sample audit of lower-value procurements.
      • Special audit in case of complaints or red flags.

    - Findings:
      • Non-conformances reported to management.
      • Corrective and preventive actions (CAPA).
      • Follow-up audit to verify closure.

6.4 EXTERNAL AUDIT AND REGULATORY COMPLIANCE

    - Statutory Auditors:
      • Review major procurement as part of financial audit.
      • Report material weaknesses or irregularities.

    - Government Audit (for PSUs, aided organizations):
      • CAG audit or departmental audit.
      • Compliance with GFR (General Financial Rules).

    - Vigilance (for PSUs):
      • CVC guidelines compliance.
      • Transparency and fairness in procurement.

7.3 JOINT / COUNTER-SIGNATURE REQUIREMENTS

    - High-Risk Procurement:
      • Joint approval by Business + Finance + Legal (above Rs. 10 Cr).

    - Single Source:
      • Procurement Head + one level higher authority.

    - Deviation from L1:
      • Procurement Head + CFO (if deviation cost > Rs. 10 lakhs).

7.4 DELEGATION PRINCIPLES

    - Clear and Unambiguous:
      • Written DoA matrix published and accessible.
      • Regular updates as organization structure changes.

    - No Unauthorized Sub-Delegation:
      • Authority holder cannot further delegate without explicit permission.

    - Accountability:
      • Authority comes with responsibility for outcomes.


SECTION 8: VENDOR PERFORMANCE AND RELATIONSHIP MANAGEMENT
================================================================================

8.1 VENDOR PERFORMANCE EVALUATION (VPE)

    - Evaluation Parameters:
      ┌────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
      │ Parameter          │ Weightage │ Scoring Criteria (1-5 scale) │
      ├────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
      │ Quality            │ 30%       │ Defect rate, rework, rejections│
      │ Delivery/Timeliness│ 25%       │ On-time delivery, delays      │
      │ Cost               │ 20%       │ Price competitiveness, extras │
      │ Responsiveness     │ 15%       │ Communication, issue resolution│
      │ Compliance         │ 10%       │ Docs, safety, statutory       │
      └────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

    - Frequency:
      • Quarterly for active vendors.
      • Annual summary for strategic vendors.

    - Rating Scale:
      • A (>4.0): Excellent, eligible for preferred status.
      • B (3.0-4.0): Satisfactory, continue as regular vendor.
      • C (2.0-3.0): Below expectations, corrective action required.
      • D (<2.0): Unacceptable, consider exit or blacklist.

8.2 VENDOR DEVELOPMENT

    - For Strategic Categories:
      • Joint improvement initiatives (quality, cost, delivery).
      • Capability building (training, process improvement).
      • Co-innovation and new product development.

    - For Underperforming Vendors:
      • Performance improvement plan (PIP).
      • Root cause analysis and corrective actions.
      • Review after 3-6 months.

8.3 STRATEGIC VENDOR PARTNERSHIPS

    - Long-Term Contracts:
      • 3-5 year rate contracts with annual price review.
      • Volume commitments (if feasible).
      • Shared risk-reward mechanisms.

    - Benefits:
      • Supply security and priority allocation.
      • Better pricing due to volume and long-term relationship.
      • Joint investment in R&D or capacity.


SECTION 9: RISK MANAGEMENT IN PROCUREMENT
================================================================================

9.1 PROCUREMENT RISK CATEGORIES

    A. SUPPLY RISK:
       - Vendor bankruptcy or business exit.
       - Capacity constraints leading to delays.
       - Quality issues or non-conformance.
       - Geopolitical risks (for imports).

    B. PRICE RISK:
       - Commodity price volatility.
       - Currency fluctuation (for imports).
       - Inflation and escalation.

    C. OPERATIONAL RISK:
       - Dependency on single vendor.
       - Long lead times.
       - Complex logistics and customs clearance.

    D. COMPLIANCE RISK:
       - Non-compliance with tax, labor, safety regulations.
       - Ethical violations (child labor, environmental damage).
       - IP infringement.

    E. REPUTATIONAL RISK:
       - Association with vendors involved in scandals.
       - Poor sustainability practices.

9.2 RISK MITIGATION STRATEGIES

    - Dual Sourcing:
      • Multiple vendors for critical items.
      • Geographic diversification.

    - Performance Bonds and Guarantees:
      • Financial security for high-value/high-risk contracts.

    - Price Hedging:
      • Forward contracts for commodities.
      • Currency hedging for imports.

    - Vendor Pre-Qualification and Monitoring:
      • Financial health checks.
      • Regular performance reviews.

    - Inventory Buffers:
      • Safety stock for critical, long-lead-time items.


SECTION 10: TECHNOLOGY AND DIGITAL PROCUREMENT
================================================================================

10.1 E-PROCUREMENT SYSTEM

    - Features:
      • Vendor registration portal.
      • Online RFQ/tender creation and publishing.
      • Electronic bid submission (technical and commercial).
      • System-generated bid opening log (audit trail).
      • Evaluation workflow with approvals.
      • PO/WO generation and tracking.
      • Integration with ERP for budget check, GRN, payment.

    - Benefits:
      • Transparency and reduced manual intervention.
      • Faster cycle time.
      • Better documentation and audit trail.
      • Data analytics for spend visibility.

10.2 SPEND ANALYTICS

    - Dashboard Metrics:
      • Category-wise spend (works, goods, services).
      • Vendor concentration (top 10 vendors by value).
      • Compliance metrics (% competitive procurement).
      • Cycle time (PR to PO).
      • Savings achieved (budgeted vs. actual).

    - Use Cases:
      • Identify cost reduction opportunities.
      • Vendor rationalization.
      • Forecast and budget planning.

10.3 VENDOR PORTAL

    - Self-Service for Vendors:
      • Registration and document submission.
      • RFQ download and bid upload.
      • Order acknowledgment and tracking.
      • Invoice submission and payment status.

    - Benefits:
      • Reduced manual coordination.
      • Better vendor experience.
      • Real-time status visibility.


SECTION 11: DISPUTE RESOLUTION AND GRIEVANCE HANDLING
================================================================================

11.1 VENDOR GRIEVANCES

    - Common Issues:
      • Delay in payments.
      • Unfair disqualification from tender.
      • Arbitrary change in specifications.
      • Non-release of retention money.

    - Grievance Mechanism:
      • Email / online portal for complaints.
      • Acknowledgment within 2 working days.
      • Investigation and response within 15 days.
      • Escalation to senior management if unresolved.

11.2 INTERNAL DISPUTES

    - Between User Dept and Procurement:
      • Specification disagreements.
      • Approval delays.
      • Vendor selection differences.

    - Resolution:
      • Escalation matrix defined.
      • Cross-functional review meeting.
      • Final decision by senior management.

11.3 LEGAL DISPUTES

    - Contract Disputes:
      • Quality, delivery, payment disputes with vendors.
      • Mediation, arbitration, or litigation as per contract.

    - Procurement Process Challenges:
      • Legal challenge by unsuccessful bidder.
      • Stay orders, court cases.
      • Legal team involvement and documentation.
