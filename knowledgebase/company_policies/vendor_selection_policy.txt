SECTION 1: OBJECTIVES, SCOPE, AND DEFINITIONS
================================================================================

1.1 POLICY OBJECTIVES

    This comprehensive vendor selection policy aims to:

    A. STRATEGIC OBJECTIVES:
       - Build a robust, reliable, and competitive vendor base across all categories
       - Ensure continuity of supply and minimize supply chain disruptions
       - Foster long-term partnerships with high-performing strategic vendors
       - Support organizational goals (cost optimization, quality, innovation, ESG)
       - Mitigate vendor-related risks (financial, operational, reputational, cyber)

    B. OPERATIONAL OBJECTIVES:
       - Establish transparent, fair, and merit-based vendor selection processes
       - Standardize vendor evaluation criteria and scoring methodologies
       - Reduce cycle time for vendor onboarding while ensuring due diligence
       - Enable data-driven vendor decisions through performance analytics
       - Maintain compliance with legal, regulatory, and ethical requirements

    C. GOVERNANCE OBJECTIVES:
       - Define clear roles, responsibilities, and approval authorities
       - Ensure audit trail and documentation for all vendor decisions
       - Prevent fraud, corruption, conflict of interest, and favoritism
       - Support internal/external audits and regulatory inspections

    A. VENDOR CATEGORIES COVERED:

1.3 KEY DEFINITIONS

    - Vendor: Any external entity providing goods, works, or services
    - Pre-Qualification (PQ): Initial screening based on capability criteria
    - Empanelment: Approved vendor list for specific category/value range
    - Technical Evaluation: Assessment of compliance with technical specifications
    - Commercial Evaluation: Assessment of pricing, payment terms, delivery
    - L1: Lowest evaluated responsive bidder (price + other factors)
    - Strategic Vendor: Long-term partner for critical categories (top 10-20%)
    - Preferred Vendor: Consistently high-performing vendor (top 30%)
    - Approved Vendor: Met minimum qualification, eligible to bid
    - Blacklisted Vendor: Debarred due to serious misconduct/breach

SECTION 3: VENDOR REGISTRATION AND ONBOARDING
================================================================================

3.1 VENDOR REGISTRATION PROCESS

    A. SELF-REGISTRATION (ONLINE PORTAL):
       - Vendor accesses organization's procurement portal
       - Creates account with basic details (name, contact, category)
       - Uploads mandatory documents (detailed in 3.2)
       - Submits online declaration and undertakings
       - Registration ID generated automatically

    B. INITIAL SCREENING (PROCUREMENT TEAM):
       - Documents verified within 7 working days
       - Completeness check, authenticity verification
       - Preliminary eligibility assessment
       - Status: Approved / Pending / Rejected (with reasons)

    C. CATEGORY ASSIGNMENT:
       - Vendor tagged to relevant procurement categories
       - Multiple categories allowed based on capability
       - Value bands assigned (Micro / Small / Medium / Large)

    D. VENDOR DATABASE ENTRY:
       - Approved vendors added to Vendor Master Database
       - Unique Vendor Code assigned (e.g., V-2026-WORKS-001)
       - Status: Registered (not yet empanelled for bidding)

4.2 SCORING CRITERIA - DETAILED POINT SYSTEM

    For scored evaluation (typically technical + commercial 70:30 or 80:20):

    A. TECHNICAL SCORING (100 points typical allocation):

       1. EXPERIENCE AND TRACK RECORD (30 points):
          - Years in business:
            • <5 years: 0 points
            • 5-10 years: 10 points
            • 10-15 years: 20 points
            • >15 years: 30 points

          - Number of similar projects (last 5 years):
            • 0-2 projects: 0 points
            • 3-5 projects: 10 points
            • 6-10 projects: 20 points
            • >10 projects: 30 points

          - Total value of work executed (last 5 years):
            • < Contract value: 0 points
            • 1-2x: 10 points
            • 2-5x: 20 points
            • >5x: 30 points

       2. TECHNICAL CAPABILITY (25 points):
          - Quality certifications:
            • No certification: 0 points
            • ISO 9001: 10 points
            • ISO 9001 + sector-specific (e.g., ISO 14001, 45001): 15 points
            • Above + advanced (e.g., Six Sigma, TQM): 20 points

          - Technical resources (qualified personnel):
            • Minimum required: 5 points
            • 1.5x minimum: 10 points
            • 2x minimum: 15 points

          - Equipment and facilities:
            • Minimum adequate: 5 points
            • Well-equipped: 10 points
            • State-of-art: 15 points

       3. METHODOLOGY AND APPROACH (20 points):
          - Project execution plan quality: 0-10 points
          - Resource deployment plan: 0-5 points
          - Risk mitigation strategy: 0-5 points

       4. DELIVERY CAPABILITY (15 points):
          - Proposed delivery schedule vs requirement:
            • Meets requirement: 5 points
            • 10% better: 10 points
            • 20%+ better: 15 points

          - Past delivery performance (reference checks):
            • Frequent delays: 0 points
            • Occasional delays: 5 points
            • On-time delivery >80%: 10 points
            • On-time delivery >95%: 15 points

       5. COMPLIANCE AND CERTIFICATIONS (10 points):
          - Statutory compliances current: 5 points
          - Safety record clean: 3 points
          - Environmental compliance: 2 points

       MINIMUM TECHNICAL SCORE TO QUALIFY: 70/100 (adjust per project)

    B. COMMERCIAL SCORING (100 points):

       1. PRICE COMPETITIVENESS (70 points):
          - Formula-based scoring:
            Score = (Lowest Price / Bidder's Price) × 70

          Example:
          L1 Bidder: Rs. 100 Cr → (100/100) × 70 = 70 points
          L2 Bidder: Rs. 105 Cr → (100/105) × 70 = 66.67 points
          L3 Bidder: Rs. 110 Cr → (100/110) × 70 = 63.64 points

       2. PAYMENT TERMS (15 points):
          - Advance required:
            • >20%: 0 points
            • 10-20%: 5 points
            • <10%: 10 points
            • No advance: 15 points

          - Credit period offered:
            • <30 days: 0 points
            • 30 days: 5 points
            • 45 days: 10 points
            • 60+ days: 15 points

       3. WARRANTY AND SUPPORT (10 points):
          - Warranty period:
            • Minimum (1 year): 3 points
            • Standard (2 years): 5 points
            • Extended (3-5 years): 8 points
            • >5 years: 10 points

          - After-sales support:
            • Basic (email/phone): 2 points
            • On-site support: 5 points
            • 24x7 support + spares: 10 points

       4. VALUE-ADDED SERVICES (5 points):
          - Training included: 2 points
          - Spare parts (initial stock): 2 points
          - Performance guarantees: 1 point

       TOTAL COMBINED SCORE = (Technical Score × 70%) + (Commercial Score × 30%)

       Example Calculation:
       Bidder A: Technical 85/100, Commercial 70/100
       Combined = (85 × 0.7) + (70 × 0.3) = 59.5 + 21 = 80.5

       Bidder B: Technical 75/100, Commercial 90/100 (L1)
       Combined = (75 × 0.7) + (90 × 0.3) = 52.5 + 27 = 79.5

       Winner: Bidder A (highest combined score, not L1)

4.3 REFERENCE CHECKS

    Minimum 3 references required (5 for critical/high-value):

    Reference Verification Format:
    - Client name, designation, contact
    - Project details (scope, value, duration)
    - Performance rating (1-5 scale):
      • Quality of work
      • Timeliness (adherence to schedule)
      • Cost management (variation from contract)
      • Safety and compliance
      • Responsiveness and cooperation
      • Overall satisfaction
    - Would you hire this vendor again? (Yes/No/Maybe)
    - Any unresolved disputes or claims?

    Red Flags in References:
    - Rating below 3/5 in any category
    - "No" to re-hiring question
    - Pending litigation or disputes
    - Safety incidents or fatalities
    - Significant cost/time overruns

4.4 SITE/FACILITY VISIT

    For high-risk/high-value vendors, conduct site visit to verify:
    - Physical existence and scale of operations
    - Manufacturing/storage/office facilities
    - Equipment and machinery (condition, capacity)
    - Quality control systems and labs
    - Safety practices and housekeeping
    - Worker conditions and welfare
    - Stock of materials/finished goods
    - IT systems and automation level

    Visit Report includes photos, observations, and recommendation (Approve/Reject/Conditional).


SECTION 5: EMPANELMENT PROCESS
================================================================================

5.1 EMPANELMENT vs PROJECT-SPECIFIC SELECTION

    A. EMPANELMENT:
       - Vendor pre-approved for a category for defined period (1-3 years)
       - Faster project execution (skip PQ, directly invite for bidding)
       - Annual/periodic review of performance
       - Benefits: Reduced cycle time, relationship building

       Suitable For:
       - Repetitive procurement categories
       - Framework/rate contracts
       - Emergency procurement readiness

    B. PROJECT-SPECIFIC SELECTION:
       - Fresh evaluation for each project/tender
       - No prior empanelment required
       - Full PQ + technical + commercial evaluation each time

       Suitable For:
       - Large, unique, one-time projects
       - Specialized requirements
       - When empanelled vendors insufficient

5.2 EMPANELMENT PROCESS WORKFLOW

    Step 1: Empanelment Notification
       - Public advertisement (website, newspapers for critical categories)
       - Empanelment criteria document published
       - Application submission deadline (typically 30-60 days)

    Step 2: Application Submission
       - Vendors submit detailed application with documents
       - Application fee (if applicable): Rs. _____
       - Acknowledgment issued

    Step 3: Preliminary Screening
       - Completeness check (7 days)
       - Incomplete applications rejected or clarifications sought

    Step 4: Detailed Evaluation
       - Technical evaluation team assesses against criteria
       - Financial analysis (ratios, creditworthiness)
       - Reference checks and site visits (if required)
       - Evaluation report prepared (14-21 days)

    Step 5: Approval
       - Empanelment Committee reviews and approves
       - Committee composition:
         • Procurement Head (Chair)
         • Finance representative
         • Technical/Operations head
         • Legal (for high-risk)
       - Decision: Approve / Reject / Conditional Approval

    Step 6: Empanelment Letter
       - Empanelled vendors issued formal letter
       - Validity period, category, value limits specified
       - Terms and conditions of empanelment
       - Performance expectations and review mechanism

    Step 7: Periodic Review
       - Annual performance review
       - Renewal / De-empanelment / Upgrade-Downgrade decision

5.3 EMPANELMENT CATEGORIES AND TIERS

    Vendors empanelled in tiers based on capability:

    ┌────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐

    ├────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
    │ A    │ Large, experienced │ No limit           │ 20%          │
    │ B    │ Medium capability  │ Up to Rs. 50 Cr    │ 30%          │

    - Tier C: Eligible for small projects, capacity building opportunities

6.3 PERFORMANCE IMPROVEMENT PLAN (PIP)

    For vendors rated C or below:

    Step 1: Performance Review Meeting (within 15 days of rating)
       - Discuss performance gaps with vendor
       - Root cause analysis
       - Vendor's explanation and commitments

    Step 2: PIP Development (within 7 days)
       - Specific improvement targets (SMART goals)
       - Timelines (typically 3-6 months)
       - Support from organization (if needed, e.g., training)
       - Monitoring mechanism (weekly/monthly reviews)

    Step 3: Implementation and Monitoring
       - Regular progress reviews
       - Corrective actions tracked
       - Interim assessments

    Step 4: Final Assessment
       - Re-evaluation at end of PIP period
       - Outcomes:
         • Improved: Restore normal status
         • Marginal improvement: Extend PIP
         • No improvement: De-empanelment/blacklisting

6.4 VENDOR DEVELOPMENT PROGRAM

    For strategic/preferred vendors, organization may invest in:
    - Joint process improvement initiatives (Kaizen, Lean, Six Sigma)
    - Technology upgrades (co-investment in equipment)
    - Training and capacity building
    - Quality system certifications (ISO sponsorship)
    - Financial support (advance payment, equipment financing)
    - Long-term contracts with volume commitments

    Benefits: Supply chain resilience, innovation, cost reduction, loyalty


SECTION 7: BLACKLISTING AND DE-EMPANELMENT
================================================================================

7.1 GROUNDS FOR BLACKLISTING

    A. CRITICAL VIOLATIONS (Immediate blacklisting):
       - Fraud, forgery, or misrepresentation of documents
       - Bribery, corruption, or kickbacks
       - Criminal activities or conviction
       - Willful breach causing major loss/damage
       - Submission of fake BG, insurance, or certificates

    B. SERIOUS VIOLATIONS (Blacklisting after show-cause):
       - Persistent poor quality (multiple failures/rejections)
       - Repeated delivery failures (>3 times in 12 months)
       - Abandonment of contract without valid reason
       - Serious safety incident due to negligence (fatality)
       - Environmental violations causing significant damage
       - Breach of confidentiality or IP theft
       - Statutory non-compliance (PF, ESI fraud)

    C. PERFORMANCE-BASED (After warnings and PIP failure):
       - Consistent D rating for 2 consecutive periods
       - Failure to improve despite PIP
       - Bankruptcy/insolvency proceedings

7.2 BLACKLISTING PROCEDURE

    Step 1: Notice of Proposed Blacklisting (NOPB)
       - Issued by Procurement Head
       - Details of allegations/grounds
       - Supporting documents/evidence
       - Opportunity to respond within 21 days

    Step 2: Vendor's Response
       - Written explanation and defense
       - Counter-evidence, if any
       - Request for personal hearing (if desired)

    Step 3: Inquiry (if required)
       - Inquiry committee formed (3-5 members)
       - Examination of evidence
       - Personal hearing to vendor
       - Inquiry report with findings and recommendation

    Step 4: Decision by Competent Authority
       - Review of inquiry report
       - Decision to blacklist or not
       - If blacklisted:
         • Period of blacklisting (1 year to permanent)
         • Scope (specific category or all categories)
         • Communication to all business units

    Step 5: Notification and Recording
       - Blacklist order issued to vendor
       - Entry in central blacklist register
       - Circulation to industry associations (if serious fraud)
       - Update in vendor database (status: BLACKLISTED)

    Step 6: Appeal (if policy permits)
       - Vendor may appeal to higher authority within 30 days
       - Appeal reviewed by independent committee
       - Final decision communicated

7.3 CONSEQUENCES OF BLACKLISTING

    - No participation in any tenders/quotations
    - Termination of ongoing contracts (if clause permits)
    - Forfeiture of security deposits and BGs
    - No payment for work done (if fraud established)
    - Recovery of damages/losses
    - Legal action for fraud/criminal acts
    - Information sharing with other organizations (if permitted by policy)

7.4 REHABILITATION

    - After blacklist period expiry, vendor may apply for re-registration
    - Fresh evaluation as new vendor
    - Enhanced scrutiny and verification
    - Probationary period (e.g., limited value contracts for 1 year)


SECTION 8: SPECIAL PROVISIONS
================================================================================

8.1 MSME VENDOR PREFERENCE

    In line with MSME Development Act, 2006:

    A. RESERVATION:
       - Target: Minimum 25% of total procurement from MSMEs
       - Sub-target: 3% from SC/ST MSMEs (if applicable)

    B. PRICE PREFERENCE:
       - If MSME within 15% of L1 (non-MSME), MSME gets preference
       - Example:
         L1 (Large): Rs. 100 Lakhs
         L2 (MSME): Rs. 112 Lakhs → Within 15% (Rs. 115 L) → Award to MSME

    C. PAYMENT TERMS:
       - Payment within 45 days mandatory (MSME Act)
       - Interest @ 3 times Bank Rate if delayed beyond 45 days
       - Advance payment up to 20% without BG (if policy permits)

    D. VERIFICATION:
       - MSME certificate verified from Udyam portal
       - Validity and authenticity checked

8.2 LOCAL CONTENT AND INDIGENOUS PREFERENCE

    - "Make in India" / "Atmanirbhar Bharat" alignment
    - Local content requirements as per project/policy:
      • Minimum __% local content (materials, labor, manufacturing)
    - Verification through self-declaration + certificates
    - Price preference (up to 20%) for domestic manufacturers over imports

8.3 SUSTAINABLE AND GREEN PROCUREMENT

    Preference for vendors demonstrating:
    - Environmental certifications (ISO 14001, green labels)
    - Sustainable sourcing (FSC for timber, fair trade, etc.)
    - Energy-efficient products (BEE 5-star ratings)
    - Low carbon footprint, recyclability
    - Social responsibility (fair labor, no child labor)

    Scoring Benefit: Additional 5-10% weightage in technical evaluation

8.4 START-UPS AND INNOVATION

    - Special empanelment window for start-ups and innovators
    - Relaxed eligibility (lower turnover, experience requirements)
    - Pilot/trial orders to test new products/solutions
    - Mentorship and incubation support (if organization has CSR/innovation program)
